Congratulations on getting your teams/selves signed up for the 2016 Gigazone Gaming Championship! We just wanted to reach out with this email to let you all know what the plan is from here on out. First off, we have 19 teams and 52 individual players registered for the championship. That's a great turn out! We can't express how exciting it is to have a gaming tournament in our area and to have so many players ready to game!

In the following weeks we will be taking lone wolf registrants and placing them in empty slots on the teams we have registered. Team captains, we'll be emailing you to let you know who has been placed on your teams so that you can get a hold of them and start practicing as soon as possible. Lone wolves, we'll also be emailing you the contact info for the captains of the teams you're being assigned to so you can start that dialog as well.

Please remember that LoL IS A TEAM SPORT! You may not get to play the position or character you want to. It's up to you to mesh with your team and learn how to work together if you want to win! After all the empty spots in registered teams are filled, we will then be taking the remaining lone wolves and assigning them to 5 man/woman teams. In a perfect world, everyone will be placed on a team, but it's more than likely that we might have a couple of players who just don't fit. We will be offering those few remaining lone wolves spots as alternates. Thus, if a player or 2 drops before game day, we can then shuffle those lone wolves into those newly empty spots. All placement of lone wolf applicants will be RANDOMLY placed on teams, so if you're not placed please understand that it's nothing personal! Just hold onto the hope of being placed if/when another player drops.

With that said, we want to remind everyone that this championship is only open to CURRENT RESIDENTS OF THE 218 AREA CODE. If you have signed up and do not live in the 218 area code, please let us know so that we can drop you off of the roster of possible players and get the empty slots filled with resident participants. While we do hope to open up further championships to competitors throughout the state in the future, we had to stick to only current residents of the 218 area code for this one. On game day (November 12, 2016) only 8 teams will be competing at the . What this means to you as competitors is that we will be hosting our preliminary games online, likely using a service called . We're sure many of you are already familiar with the Battlefy service and are aware just how easy it is to use. We'll have more details on that for you as time goes on. While we will not be verifying your current residence during the preliminary games, we will be checking photo IDs on game day of either you as the player (if aged 18 or older) or of your parents if you are under the age of 18. Either way, we will need some proof of residence come game day. If you cannot provide that proof at that time then you will be disqualified from participation.

We will try to schedule the preliminary games as conveniently for both teams as we can to ensure that everyone has a chance to play them. We plan on communicating date and time options for the teams ahead of time so that competing teams are able to make their games against each other. Ideally, the preliminary games will be taking place during evening hours, so hopefully everyone will be able to make some time to play from home. You'll hear more from us soon as the preliminaries draw near.

Outside of that, welcome to the 2016 Gigazone Gaming Championship everyone! Good luck!
